The Journey

Born and raised in Milledgeville Georgia and gifted with a powerful drive and an inquisitive mindset. Not knowing where these gifts would lead me, I pursued many interests until fate lead me to Computers. My first experience was with Game Development and unfortunately due to my lack of experience and knowledge, I failed. Unknowingly, I found this failure as the spark needed to ignite the passion I now have for Computer Science. I worked for 1yr independently on a simple racing game with no computer experience or a firm understanding of the fundamental concepts of any subjects involved. Nearing completion the computer I was working on failed on me and all progression was lost. I was down but not out.

The thought of the outcome of the project haunted me. Not because it failed, but what could I have done to better my experience? One day I met a Computer Science Professor, Denis Masters whom open the door to the answers I seeked. When we first met I talked about my project and the objectives that I wanted to accomplish. He spoke of the wonders of Computer Science and with my drive I would find the correct path. This inspired me, I Changed my major and the real journey began. From Georgia Military College to Georgia College and State University I have faced many exciting and tedious challeges gaining unmeasurable amounts of knowledge about preperation,foucusing, and most importantaly computers. My interests had grown, giving me a whole new world to look forward to.

My journey to becoming as a CS Major has been both challenging and rewarding. During my first year as a Computer Science Major, I faced numerous hurdles as I struggled to grasp the fundamental concepts of programming and computer systems. However, with the support of patient professors and dedicated classmates, I gradually began to build my understanding of coding languages like Python and Java. As I progressed through the CS program, I participated in various extracurricular activities,and coding clubs to enhance my skills and broaden my horizons. I found mentors who guided me through complex topics like data structures, algorithms, and software engineering practices. In my junior year, I got my first taste of machine learning and data analysis, which captivated me with its potential to solve real-world problems. Despite being intimidated at first, I dove into online courses and personal projects to explore this exciting domain. I also dabbled in web development, learning about front-end technologies like HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, and back-end technologies like Node.js and databases.
